Kosgoda hatchery



Mission and humble beginning


A little seaside town called Kosgoda may be found in Sri Lanka's southwest. Its goal is to safeguard sea turtles and their environments, and it is renowned for its turtle conservation initiatives. The modest beginnings of turtle conservation in Kosgoda can be linked to the initiatives of regional groups and people who saw how crucial it was to protect these imperiled species.



Its conservation effects


The conservation efforts in Kosgoda have contributed to the recovery of sea turtle populations, ecosystem balance, community involvement and livelihoods, environmental education and awareness, and sustainable tourism. These efforts have enabled the local community to work as guides, caretakers, or volunteers at the turtle hatchery, fostering a sense of ownership and pride in protecting their natural heritage. They have also raised awareness about the threats faced by sea turtles and promoted responsible tourism practices, such as respecting nesting sites and not disturbing turtles.
